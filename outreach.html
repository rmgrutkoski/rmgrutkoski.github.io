Outreach

I've had many wonderful experiences in science outreach. During my undergrad, I volunteered with and later worked as a co-director of a middle school science outreach program, which ran two programs a semester, BLAST and ATHENAS. The program originally started as a way to reach girls and provide opportunities for them to explore science, engaging their curiosity and excitement. It later expanded to serve many students in rural areas who otherwise had little chance to get hands-on experience with a wide range of science.

Mentoring is also an incredibly important aspect of science and our community. In undergrad, I volunteered as a STEM mentor, working especially with first-year students. During my senior year of undergrad, in addition to my role as a mentor within the program, I also served as one of the student coordinators, helping with recruitment of mentors and mentees, facilitating training and group activities, and working with affinity groups to provide an extra layer of support for minority groups within STEM.
